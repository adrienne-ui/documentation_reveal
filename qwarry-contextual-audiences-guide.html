Qwarry Contextual Audiences

User Guide — How it works, what it targets, and what it's composed of

1. What are Contextual Audiences?

Contextual Audiences are groups of users (IDs) who have visited web pages whose content has been semantically analyzed and categorized by Qwarry's technology. Unlike traditional cookie-based audiences, they are built from the context of the content consumed, not from personal data.

In simple terms: if a user visits pages about cooking, gardening, and healthy eating, they become part of contextual audiences related to those topics — without any personal data collection.

Key principle: A Contextual Audience is made up of people (IDs) that have visited URLs categorized by Qwarry. These IDs browse the internet and can appear on many different types of content — they are not limited to themed sites.

2. Contextual Audiences vs Contextual Categories

These are two distinct products that serve different purposes:

Contextual Audiences

  • Made up of: People (IDs) that have visited a URL categorized by Qwarry
  • What you target: A user who showed interest in a topic
  • Behavior: IDs browse the internet and can appear on any type of content
  • Sitelist: Not recommended — using a sitelist limits the chances of reaching the user again

Contextual Categories

  • Made up of: URLs that Qwarry's technology has categorized
  • What you target: A page that talks about a specific topic
  • Behavior: A list of URLs about the targeted topic
  • Sitelist: OK — can be combined with themed sitelists
Important: Do not use a themed sitelist with Contextual Audiences. Since you are targeting a user who showed interest (not a page), restricting to specific sites dramatically reduces your reach.

3. How Contextual Audiences Are Created

The creation process follows 5 steps:

Step 1 — Xandr Sends Data

Xandr provides Qwarry with URLs and the IDs of users who visited those URLs.

Step 2 — Content Analysis

Qwarry's technology analyzes the URLs to:

  • Identify the content — Semantic analysis of the page text (crawling, extracting, scoring)
  • Construct Semos — Assign semantic categories (e.g. Agriculture, Food & Drink, Medical Health)

A URL Selector tool prioritizes which URLs to score first based on:

  • Cross-partner intelligence — URLs requested by multiple partners are prioritized
  • Domain authority — Pre-classified high-priority websites bypass the queue
  • Freshness — URLs less than 72 hours old are processed first
  • Brand safety filtering — Obvious toxic content is rejected before scoring

Step 3 — Audience Construction

The categorized URLs are matched with the IDs that visited them, creating the Contextual Audience:

URLs (categorized) + IDs (visitors) = Contextual Audience

Recency limit: Data is retained for 90 days for storage and cost reasons. Audiences cannot be calculated with a recency greater than 90 days.

Step 4 — Sending to Xandr

The constructed Contextual Audience (containing IDs) is sent back to Xandr for activation in campaigns.

Step 5 — Cross-Device Matching

Using the cross-device targeting feature, web IDs are matched to other environments via Microsoft's Identity Graph:

  • Apps
  • Audio
  • CTV (Connected TV)

Visual Flow

Xandr sends URLs + IDs → Qwarry analyzes content → Semos assigned → URLs + IDs = Audience → Sent to Xandr → Cross-device matching

4. What is a Contextual Audience Composed Of?

Each Contextual Audience is defined by the following parameters:

ParameterDescription
NameDescriptive name of the audience
SegmentsThe Semos (semantic categories) associated to the audience. One audience can combine multiple segments (from 1 to 100+).
FrequencyHow often a user must have visited relevant content to qualify (e.g. 1, 3, 5, 10, 15, 20 visits)
RecencyHow recently the user must have visited (e.g. 1 day, 7 days, 30 days, 60 days — max 90 days)
CountryGeographic scope — can be single or multi-country (e.g. FR, DE, ES, GB, CA, IT, CH, etc.)

5. Targeting & Context

What You're Targeting

With Contextual Audiences, you target a person who has demonstrated interest in a topic through their browsing behavior. This is fundamentally different from contextual categories where you target a page.

The Context

The "context" comes from Qwarry's semantic analysis of web pages. The technology reads and understands the content of URLs to assign them to categories (Semos). Examples of Semos include:

  • Agriculture, Food & Drink, Medical Health, Home & Garden
  • Business and Finance, Family and Relationships, Pop Culture
  • Healthy Living, Personal Finance, Television

How Targeting Works in Practice

  1. A user visits pages about "organic gardening" and "sustainable living"
  2. Qwarry categorizes those pages under relevant Semos
  3. The user's ID is added to the corresponding Contextual Audience (e.g. "Ecologist")
  4. When that user later browses any website (news, sports, entertainment...), your ad can reach them
Key advantage: You reach users based on their proven interests, regardless of what page they are currently on. This provides much broader reach than contextual category targeting while maintaining relevance.
